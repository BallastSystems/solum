How it works

$SOLUM launches on pump.fun. pump.fun pays creator fees on every trade — and we wire those fees straight into an hourly stock raffle for holders.

1

Hold $SOLUM

Buy and hold like any Solana memecoin. Your average balance over the hour is your entry — no staking, no lock-up.

2

Fees buy real stock, every hour

Each hour the accrued creator fees automatically buy real tokenized stock into the pot — the bigger the trading volume, the bigger the buy.

3

A holder wins the pot

At a random moment each hour, one holder is drawn to win the whole pot of real stock. The more $SOLUM you hold, the higher your odds. You keep your coins either way.

Provably fair

The draw isn't a promise — it's code you can read. Randomness comes from a Switchboard oracle no one can predict or grind, and the winner is settled on-chain.

Randomness you can't rig

The winning ticket comes straight from a Switchboard VRF — revealed by an oracle network. Not the team, not a bot, not the winner can predict or influence it.

You can't game the snapshot

Your odds are your average $SOLUM balance over the whole hour, and the draw fires at a random moment — so buying a big bag right before does nothing, and you can't even know when to try.

More $SOLUM, better odds

Tickets scale linearly with how much you held. Simple, transparent, and every line is open-source.

fn winning_ticket(randomness: &[u8; 32], total: u64) -> u64 {
    u64::from_le_bytes(randomness[..8]) % total
}
