From snapshot to winner

One hidden snapshot, a 5-minute countdown, verifiable randomness. Here's exactly how each hourly draw runs — and why no one, us included, can bend it.

1

Snapshot, sealed on-chain

At a hidden, random moment each hour, your time-averaged $SOLUM balance becomes tickets and the full list is locked on-chain. A last-second bag earns almost nothing — and once sealed, entries can't change.

2

Drawn by Switchboard VRF

Five minutes after the snapshot — a public countdown — a verifiable-random oracle produces a number no one, team, bot, or winner, can predict or grind. The winner is whoever holds that ticket.

3

Verify it yourself

Fair by design

The draw isn't a promise — it's code you can read. Randomness comes from a Switchboard oracle no one can predict or grind, and the winner is settled on-chain.

Randomness you can't rig

The winning ticket comes straight from a Switchboard VRF — revealed by an oracle network. Not the team, not a bot, not the winner can predict or influence it.

You can't game the snapshot

Both the snapshot and the draw happen at random, unannounced times every hour, and your odds are your average $SOLUM balance over the hour — so you can't know when to buy, and buying a big bag right before does nothing anyway.

More $SOLUM, better odds

Tickets scale linearly with how much you held. Simple, transparent, and every line is open-source.

    u64::from_le_bytes(randomness[..8]) % total
